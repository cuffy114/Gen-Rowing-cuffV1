ARMS ONLY – Body must remain tall and still as we draw the hands to the ribs

  • Aim is to maintain pressure on foot stretcher and handle (not add more) until the finish – this will enable a clean release
  • Sitting centrally on seat – with some weight on the front axle, pelvis not rolled back
  • Sit still for longer (wheels stationary) relative to the rate

LEGS & BODY – Just past half slide, the body begins to 'swing open' and contributes to acceleration

  • Body must open horizontally and not lift
  • MAX BOAT acceleration (shaft square to hull)
  • Effort focused on pushing through the foot stretcher & body swing on handle, maintaining suspension and lightness on the seat

LEGS ONLY – Arms and body 'hang' off handle

  • 'Move the boat with your feet' – foot stretcher pressure
  • Glutes and hamstrings activate to push the knees down to ¼ slide
  • Looking to create pressure on the face of the blade as soon after entry as possible
  • Suspension through foot stretcher and handle lightens the weight on the seat

Stage 1
GENAZZANO
FCJ COLLEGE
Key Points
  • Starts after blade placement
  • Ends at ¼ slide
Key Aspects
  • Pressure and effort is applied on the foot stretcher
  • Brace the core as you place the blade into the water
  • Shins vertical, no gap between thighs and stomach, chest up above knees, eyes up, chin up
  • The wheels of the seat move continuously in and out of the catch
  • Suspension through foot stretcher and handle lightens the weight on the seat
  • Glutes & hamstrings activate to push knees down
  • Shoulders remain forward of hips
  • Stay long and loose through the arms and shoulders
Key Phrases
  • 'Brace & Place'
  • 'Hang & Push'
  • 'Eyes up, Chin up'
  • 'Long & Loose'
  • 'Move the boat with your feet'
  • 'Hips back'
Note
  • In reality, the body may start to swing at ½ slide (which is acceptable)
  • It is better to stay in Stage 1 for longer than start stage 2 too early
Stage 2
GENAZZANO
FCJ COLLEGE
Key Points
  • Starts at ¼ slide
  • Ends when legs are straight and body has swung into the finish position
Key Aspects
  • Effort focused on continuing to push through the foot stretcher as the body swings
  • Body must open horizontally and not lift or drop
  • Accelerate aggressively with max pressure on foot stretcher
  • Legs finish simultaneously when body swing finishes
Key Phrases
  • 'Long & loose'
  • 'Aggressive acceleration'
  • 'Knees down'
  • 'Swing back'
Stage 3
GENAZZANO
FCJ COLLEGE
Key Points
  • Starts with body in finish position and arms straight
  • Ends at the finish position
Key Aspects
  • Maintain handle acceleration whilst keeping max pressure on the foot stretcher
  • Body stays tall and still
  • Wheels of the seat are stationary
  • Aim is to maintain pressure on foot stretcher and handle (not add more) until the finish – this will enable a clean release
Key Phrases
  • 'Maintain foot stretcher pressure'
  • 'Stay tall, light on the seat'
  • 'Some weight on the front axle'
  • 'Draw up'
  • 'Patience'
  • 'Hold for longer'
Rhythm and Time
GENAZZANO
FCJ COLLEGE
Rhythm
  • Boat speed dictates recovery speed
  • To increase rate, we must first increase boat speed
  • Even at race pace, the athlete should feel they have time and control into the catch
  • It is crucial at higher rates that the finish is held or the rhythm will be compromised
Time
  • We must have acceleration in the drive
  • We must have time in the recovery
  • Time our effort in the drive (place before push)
  • Sit still at the release for longer (relative to the rate) to allow the boat to run
  • Time is relative!
